# Army Leader's Book
The 'Leader's Book' was developed based on a tool that I used to carry around everywhere with me. A Leader's Book is something that most, if not all, Non-Commissioned Officers had with them to assist in tracking and managing their teams, platoons, and units. The project I created consists of the basic pages that most Leader's book have—general Creeds, the General Orders, the Code of Conduct, the Army Song, Army Fitness Test (AFT) table, weapons qualification tracker. Many sections are still in development, but initial pages were developed to continue the ongoing project. I started this project with two smaller projects: the Army AFT Calculator and the Weapons Qualification Tracker. Both projects are embedded into this final one.

During my service, I had developed a PDF formatted Leader's Book with editable sections to maintain all the data I deemed necessary to improve tracking and management as a leader. I kept this on my iPad and never went anywhere without it. The idea behind this project was to convert the PDF format into a website that I could more easily edit, improve, and expand over time. Building this digitally also gave me a practical way to apply what I am learning in the classroom to something meaningful from my own experience. This project reinforced my understanding of HTML structure, CSS styling, JavaScript functions, and GitHub project development. Additional research into CSS styling and JavaScript functions was required to complete this project, further expanding my understanding. 

This project is still actively under development—as you may notice with my "Under Development..." message on several pages. The Roster, Chain of Command, NCO Support Channel, Certifications, and Individual Development pages are planned for future updates as my skills continue to grow throughout the course. The long-term goal is to produce a fully functional digital Leader's Book that any Non-Commissioned Officer could use in the field. This project represents not just a technical endeavor, but a continuation of my commitment to leadership and Soldier readiness beyond my time in uniform.
